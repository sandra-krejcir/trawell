Petra | Jordan

About Petra

Petra, the stunningly beautiful ancient city located in the mountains of southern Jordan, is the most visited tourist attraction in the country. Known as the Rose City due to the colour of the stone Petra is believed have been established in 312BC, making it one of the oldest cities in the world. To enter Petra, you need to go through a narrow gorge called the Siq, which is around 1km long. It is bound by cliffs each side which are around 8 metres high.
Petra Archaeological Park became a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1985 and along with among others the Roman Colosseum in Italy, Peru’s Machu Picchu and Great Wall of China, Petra was named one the New Seven Wonders of the World in 2007.

Places to visit

Nature | 90km from Petra

Wadi Rum desert

An ancient red-rock desert is a must-see place for the fans of Star Wars “The Rise of Skywalker” and Disney’s Aladdin movie. Besides camel and jeep rides, hiking and rock-climbing travelers can stay in the desert overnight in Bedouin Camps and can admire the stars as you’ve never seen them before!

Nature | 150km from Petra

Dead Sea

The Dead Sea is a salt water lake in the Middle East, bordered by Israel and the West Bank to the west, and Jordan to the East. It is one of the world’s saltiest bodies of water, which makes swimming similar to floating. Everyone should try it not only as it is a unique experience but also it can have many beneficial impacts on body and health.

City | 230km from Petra

Amman

Amman is the capital of Jordan and one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world with 7,000 years of recorded history. Today, the city is one of the most liberal in the Middle East boasting a modern infrastructure and a thriving tourism industry. You can see the Roman Citadel and Theatre as well as the Al-Husseini Mosque. Don’t forget to taste some delicious Jordanian food!

Local cuisine

Mansaf

It is a very traditional Jordanian dish that is served on important occasions, both happy and sad. It is made from a bread thinner than a tortilla at the bottom, on top of that there is cooked rice in a sauce and on it pieces of lamb that were slowly cooked in fermented, yogurt-like goat's milk called jameed.

Falafel

Falafel is one of the Middle East’s most popular dishes, it’s naturally vegan, healthy and incredibly delicious! Falafel is a chickpeas balls with spices that are usually deep-fried and served with hummus and tahini sauce.

THE RECIPE

Jameed

Jameed is a Beduin-Jordanian in a form of dried yoghurt made from goat's milk or sheep's milk. It is made by draining thick yoghurt of its whey, shaping it into balls, and letting it harden. Drying prevents spoilage. It is also customary to add to a sauces or soups.
